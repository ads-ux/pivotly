Case Study: Building the Website for PizzaCam Guelph, the Viral Robot Pizza Kiosk at UoG

By Mark · Overhauled.ai · May 2026 · 5 min read

PizzaCam isn't a typical restaurant website project. There's no menu to manage, no online ordering to wire up, and no reservation system. It's a 24/7 automated robot pizza kiosk at the University of Guelph that had already gone viral on TikTok before the site was built, and the challenge was making the website match the energy of what was already happening on social media.

The brief

PizzaCam Guelph is a fully automated pizza vending machine, you order, it bakes your pizza in under 3 minutes, and you grab it fresh from the machine. It's located at 149 Reynolds Walk, right beside the Athletic Centre and the Gryphons hockey arena, making it the ideal late-night option for students, post-gym crowds, and anyone on campus.

The kiosk has a built-in camera, the Pizza Cam, where customers voluntarily perform for a few seconds while their pizza cooks. That camera became the whole draw. TikTok videos of people dancing, doing impressions, and making jokes while waiting for their pizza racked up hundreds of thousands of views. CTV News Kitchener covered it. The Record wrote about it. It had real momentum.

What it didn't have was a website that captured any of that. The brief was simple: build something that felt as alive as the TikTok page, worked perfectly on a phone, and showed up when students searched for late-night food near campus.

What we built

The site needed to do three things well: explain the concept quickly to someone who'd never heard of it, show off the social proof (the viral reach, the reviews, the camera), and make it dead easy to find the kiosk.

A few specific decisions we made:

  • Mobile-first layout, almost every visitor is a student on their phone. The site was designed at mobile size first and scaled up, not the other way around.
  • Leading with the social numbers, 895K total reach, 30K likes, 8.1K shares. These numbers are legitimacy signals for a new concept that a lot of people haven't seen in person yet. They go above the fold.
  • The Pizza Cam as the hook, rather than treating the camera as a footnote, we built a section around it. The camera is why people go back. The website had to reflect that.
  • Clear location info, "beside the Athletic Centre and the Gryphons arena" means far more to a UoG student than a street address. We used the language the campus crowd actually uses.
  • Structured data for local search, full LocalBusiness schema with coordinates, opening hours (00:00–23:59 every day), payment methods, and FAQPage schema targeting the questions students actually search.

The SEO angle

For most restaurant websites, the goal is to rank for "[restaurant name] Guelph" and show up in the map pack. PizzaCam had a different opportunity, because it's genuinely novel, there are informational searches to capture too: "robot pizza Guelph", "automated pizza kiosk university of Guelph", "24/7 pizza on campus Guelph".

The page title, meta description, H1, and schema were all written to hit the most likely search phrases while sounding completely natural. The meta description alone mentions TikTok, Instagram, "24/7 robot pizza", and "University of Guelph", because those are the exact terms people use when they're trying to find out if what they saw on social media is real.

The lesson here: when a business has organic social momentum, the website's job is to convert that curiosity into action, not just exist as a digital brochure. Every element of the PizzaCam site is designed to capture the "wait, is this real?" moment and turn it into a visit.

The result

The site matches the brand: energetic, fun, campus-native. It loads fast on mobile, shows up for the right searches, and gives the social audience somewhere to land when they go looking. The Pizza Cam section has already driven shares on its own, people screenshot it and send it to friends.

It's one of the more unusual builds we've done, and one of the more satisfying. When the product is genuinely interesting, building a site that does it justice is a good problem to have.
